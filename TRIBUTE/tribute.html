MAHATMA GANDHI

The Father of our Nation

Mahatma Gandhi (October 2, 1869 to January 30, 1948) was the leader of India’s non-violent independence movement against British rule and in South Africa who advocated for the civil rights of Indians. Born in Porbandar, India, Gandhi studied law and organized boycotts against British institutions in peaceful forms of civil disobedience. He was killed by a fanatic in 1948.

Key events in Mahatma Gandhi’s life:

October 2, 1869: Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi is born in Porbandar, India. His upbringing is steeped in Vaishnavism and Jainism, emphasizing nonviolence and the belief in the eternal nature of everything in the universe.

1888–91: Gandhi studies law in London, where he meets notable figures like playwright George Bernard Shaw and social reformer Annie Besant.

1893–94: Gandhi takes a job with an Indian law firm in South Africa, where he experiences racial discrimination. He founds the Natal Indian Congress to advocate for Indian rights.

1906–15: Under Gandhi’s leadership, the Indian community in South Africa defies discriminatory laws through satyagraha (nonviolent resistance). Thousands are imprisoned, but the law is eventually abolished.

1919–24: Gandhi returns to India and continues his struggle for independence from British rule.

1942–44: During World War II, Gandhi launches the Quit India Movement, demanding immediate independence.

August 15, 1947: India gains independence, and Gandhi’s efforts contribute significantly to this historic achievement

August-December 1948: ·India dissolves into chaos and killings, as Hindus and Muslims flee for the borders of India and Pakistan.

January 30, 1948: ·Gandhi is assassinated by Nathuram Vinayuk Godse, a Hindu nationalist.
